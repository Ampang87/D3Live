PRODUCT LAUNCH

Introducing Response Extractor: The Unified LS-DYNA Analysis Platform Every Simulation Engineer Has Been Waiting For

By the d3VIEW Engineering Team · May 2026 · 5 min read

If you've spent time post-processing LS-DYNA results, you know the frustration: navigating between multiple tools, waiting for data to load, manually hunting through BINOUT files to find the responses that matter. Traditional workflows were built for a simpler era of simulation. Today's models — with hundreds of parts, thousands of connections, and complex occupant systems — demand something better.

Today we're launching Response Extractor, a fully unified analysis platform built directly into d3VIEW. It brings together everything you need — BINOUT extraction, energy flow analysis, connection validation, occupant safety scoring, and force analysis — into one fast, interactive interface.

What is Response Extractor? It's an advanced visualization and analysis platform for exploring simulation response data, plotting curves, analyzing load paths, validating connections, and reviewing occupant ratings — all without leaving d3VIEW.

Why We Built It

The existing extraction workflow had real limitations. Data lived in silos. Every analyst had their own patched-together process. Scalability suffered as simulation complexity grew. We designed Response Extractor from the ground up around five principles:

Unified environment Improved navigation Centralized plotting Faster loading Enterprise scalability

What You Can Do With It

BINOUT Extraction — Your Data, Instantly

Select your simulation, choose a branch (glstat, nodout, rbdout, secforc...), pick components and IDs, and click Plot. Results appear across organized multi-page layouts. Time-history curves, derived responses, and grouped analyses are all one click away. The searchable, interactive BINOUT browser eliminates the need to manually parse file structures.

Flow Analyzer — Understand Where Energy Goes

The Flow Analyzer is unlike anything available in legacy tools. It visualizes how internal energy propagates through your model part-by-part, window-by-window. The Stacked Bar view shows total energy absorbed over time. The Stream graph reveals load transfer as fluid ribbons. The Heatmap lets you compare part absorption across all time windows at once. The Leaders view identifies which part dominated absorption in each segment.

For a complex body-in-white model, this means moving from "something is wrong in the 30–40ms window" to "HAT3 absorbed 1.26 MJ in that window — 29.7% of total energy" in seconds.

Connections — 20+ Validation Standards Built In

Every spotweld, bolt, rivet, and section in your model can be validated automatically against more than 20 engineering criteria — including Shigley Method, AISC 360-16, RCSC 2014, SAE J429, and ASTM standards. Results are sortable by damage ratio, filterable by failure status, and can be extended with custom validation rules. The Load-Path view shows which connections bear the most load and when failures occur.

Occupants & Restraints — Full Safety Analysis

For crash safety engineers, the Occupants & Restraints module provides end-to-end ATD analysis aligned with US NCAP 2025 protocols. The Mapper auto-assigns BINOUT channels to injury criteria. The ATD table scores head, neck, thorax, femur, and tibia against thresholds. Airbag deployment is animated in real time. Seatbelt force curves are overlaid automatically.

Force Analyzer — Load Paths in Every Dimension

The Force Analyzer goes beyond simple force vs. time curves. The Load Path view constructs force chains station-by-station, showing how load transfers between sections. Motion view plots centroid trajectories in X, Y, and Z. The Spatial view animates force centroids in 2D. Gantt view shows exactly when each section exceeds force thresholds across the entire simulation duration.

How to Access It

Response Extractor is available now at app.d3view.com/tests/response.extractor.html?srs_id=YOUR_SIM_ID. Replace the simulation ID with your run ID and you're in. It's also accessible from the Utilities → Response Extraction menu inside any simulation view.

Important note on data quality: Extraction accuracy depends on the quality of your simulation files. Missing or corrupted BINOUT, D3HSP, or D3PLOT files will affect results. Ensure your simulation run completed successfully before extracting.

What's Coming Next

File Upload support (direct BINOUT upload without a simulation record), expanded LLM Agent integration for natural-language querying, and additional template configurations are on the roadmap. The features highlighted in red in the outline — File Upload and Template Configurations — are already in development.

Ready to try it? If you're an existing d3VIEW customer, log in and navigate to any simulation to get started. If you're new to d3VIEW, request a demo below and we'll walk you through Response Extractor with your own data.

Here's what "energy flow analysis" actually means in a crash model — and why it matters.

When a vehicle impacts a barrier, kinetic energy has to go somewhere. It converts to internal (strain) energy absorbed by deforming parts. Knowing which parts absorb how much, and when is the difference between a good design and a failed one.

Our new Flow Analyzer in d3VIEW Response Extractor gives you this in 5 views:

📊 Stacked Bar — total energy per time window, stacked by part
🌊 Stream — load transfer shown as fluid ribbons
🔥 Heatmap — which part was hottest in each time window
🏆 Leaders — the dominant absorber in each segment
📈 Energy Share — each part's % of total, with activation time

In our example run: HAT3 absorbed 29.7% of total energy — 7,402 kJ across 10 parts tracked. That analysis used to take hours. Now it's seconds.
